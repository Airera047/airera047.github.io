Undergraduate Research

Delineo Covid-19 Modeling Project

Institute: Johns Hopkins University   Advisor: Dr. Anton Debura

Aimed to develop a new model that more accurately understands and predicts the spread of diseases such as COVID-19 considering different types and sizes of communities and lag time of spreads across areas.

[Summer] Developed the website with MERN (Front-end prototype with Adobe XD), created data visualization for simulation result, and participated in ML algorithm brainstorm and hmm method implementation

[Fall] Implemented Markov/LSTM model, applied Geolocation data in different algorithms to discern movement patterns/anomalies within different communities, and reflected in the simulation process

PySocialForce

Institute:Intuitive Computing Lab at Johns Hopkins University

Advisor: Dr. Chien-Ming Huang   Co-Advisor: Yuxiang Gao

A Python Implementation of the Extended Social Force Model for Pedestrian Dynamics. Aimed to extend the vanilla social force model to simulate the walking behaviour of pedestrian social groups

Course:Human-Computer Interaction

Professor: Dr. Chien-Ming Huang

JHConnect aims to bridge the gap between students and professor at the Johns Hopkins University. It will serve as a platform to help students in need, especially international students, to immerse into healthier and more meaningful college lives. Sleep App for Athletes

Course:Computer Science Innovation & Entrepreneurship

Advisor: Dr. Anton Debura   Co-Advisor: Professor Lawrence Aronhime

Sleep App will essentially be composed of three functions: sleep and habit tracking, data analysis, and smart device integration. Sleep tracking will utilize either a phone or a wearable such as an Apple Watch or a Fitbit to monitor the user’s sleep quality via their heartbeat, body movement, breathing, etc.
